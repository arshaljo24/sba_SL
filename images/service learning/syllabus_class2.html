Syllabus for CLASS -2

Skill-Based

The skill-based course model for Class 2 centers on language, mathematics, and social studies, incorporating interactive and practical learning methods. In language, emphasis is placed on reading comprehension, pronunciation, and vocabulary development through activities like storytelling and role-playing. Writing skills are honed through creative exercises and dictation. Mathematics instruction includes hands-on activities for number understanding, introduction to arithmetic operations, and problem-solving. Social studies explore community roles, nature, cultural diversity, and basic geography. Integrative activities involve collaborative projects, hands-on experiments, and field trips, promoting a holistic and engaging learning experience tailored to Class 2 developmental stages. Regular assessments assess both content understanding and practical skill application.

Concept-Based

In Class 2, children become experienced writers, readers and Maths experts by developing their skills in more complex and comprehensive ways. Class 2 students read more complicated and bigger books, write in-depth and long answers, and learn more about the concepts behind certain Maths skills. They also get engaged with projects which involve research and critical thinking, individually or in groups and later share learning information through writing and speaking. Students of Class 2 learn more complex words in the form of genres, including fiction, non-fiction and poetry. Just like in the previous class, Class 2 students continuously practise reading, as they need to use texts for other subjects.
