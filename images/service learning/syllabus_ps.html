Concept-Based

Concept-based learning for PlaySchool Kids:

What is a Story?

  • Introduce classic fables like "The Lion and the Mouse" and "The Tortoise and the Hare."
  • Discuss characters, setting, and basic storyline.
Interactive Storytelling:
  • Use felt boards or puppets for story enactment.
  • Encourage participation with sound prompts.
Art Activity:
  • Draw favorite story characters.
Friendship and Teamwork:
  • Read "The Two Hungry Cats" emphasizing sharing and cooperation.
  • Play cooperative games like "The Farmer in the Dell."
  • Set up a pretend play area to encourage teamwork.
Perseverance and Overcoming Challenges:
  • Read "The Ugly Duckling" focusing on overcoming obstacles and self-acceptance.
  • Sing songs about perseverance.
  • Create a sensory play activity like a "mud puddle" to encourage problem-solving.
Cleverness and Problem Solving:
  • Read "The Monkey and the Cap Seller" discussing using intelligence to solve problems.
  • Play puzzles and matching games.
  • Make animal masks based on stories for creative play.
Review and Wrap-Up:
  • Retell favorite stories.
  • Have a dress-up day as story characters.
  • Invite parents for a story time party with refreshments and a final read-aloud session.
